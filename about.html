History of NoCaSS

NoCaSS was founded in 2013 by Annis Richardson and Jo Harrison, who were alumni of University of Cambridge and PhD students at the John Innes Centre. The founders of the conference seized upon the opportunity to connect two physically close research plant centres in a day of exchange of ideas, new collaborations, and innovations for future projects that removed the intimidation of large, international conferences for students. It helped connect the two premiere plant research centres and provide opportunities for discussion and collaboration that was sparse for students, in a day-long symposium known as Norwich-Cambridge Student Symposium. Thus, NoCaSS was born and has been held on a regular basis as a student-led and student-oriented conference.

NoCaSS was then held in Cambridge in 2014 and reiterated in Norwich in 2015. After a hiatus, NoCaSS was held in Norwich in 2017. A few more years of hiatus and an unfortunate circumstances posed by the pandemic, led the a virtual iteration of the conference hosted in Norwich in 2021.
